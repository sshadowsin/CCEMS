Ativan

Lorazepam
Approved for Rescue Paramedics Only

Protocol 1022

NOT a treatment protocol. See reference protocol listed below for use.

Reference Protocols: 201, 509, 714, 802.

Anticonvulsant / Sedative (Benzodiazepine)

MECHANISM OF ACTION

1. Suppresses Seizure Activity Through Motor Cortex of the Brain.
2. Effective Skeletal Muscle Relaxant.

INDICATIONS

1. Major Motor Seizures - Status Epilepticus.
2. Chemical Restraint/Sedative for combative patient.
3. Anxiolytic
4. Skeletal Muscle Relaxant in Isolated Orthopedic Injury.

  • Example: Femur Fracture and Severe Muscle Spasms.

5. Severe Back Pain With Prolonged Transport.

CONTRAINDICATIONS

1. Pregnancy.
2. Known Hypersensitivity.
3. Incompatible with Zofran - Flush tubing well between uses.

SIDE EFFECTS

1. Respiratory Depression/Arrest.
2. Hypotension.
3. Nausea/Vomiting.
4. Amnesia
5. Paradoxical Reactions:

  • a. Anxiety
  • b. Excitation
  • c. Agitation
  • d. Aggression
  • e. Rage-Hostility

Dosage and Administration

Lorazepam (Ativan)

  1. ADULT: 1mg IV/IM/IO initial dose and can repeat at 5 minutes - total maximum dose 2mg. If possible, contact OLMC prior to the second dose.
  2. Peds: 0.05 mg/kg IV/IM/IO with maximum initial dose of 1mg and can repeat at 5 minutes - Total maximum dose 2mg. If possible, contact OLMC prior to the second dose.
